Overview

The system runs a multi-layer intelligence pipeline every 2 minutes, aggregating signals from four independent desks: Twitter/X monitoring (Pulse), news feed scanning (Beacon), Polymarket price/volume analysis (Market), and live order book data (OrderFlow). Each desk produces signals with a direction (YES-leaning or NO-leaning), a confidence score (0–100), and an importance score (0–100). The Command module combines all active signals into a single probability estimate and a clear bet recommendation.

Step 1 — Signal Collection & Filtering

Signals are stored for a rolling 72-hour window. Before aggregation, stale signals are down-weighted. Only signals with an importance score ≥ 28 are retained for the final roll-up. Each signal carries:

FieldMeaningRange
signal_directionbullish (YES) / bearish (NO) / neutralenum
confidence_scoreHow certain we are about this signal's direction0–100
importance_scoreHow significant this signal is for the outcome0–100
probability_impact_estimateEstimated pp shift on YES price (positive = higher, negative = lower)−15 to +15
Step 2 — Desk Signal Weights

Each desk has a credibility weight that scales how much its signals move the final estimate. Pulse and Beacon are weighted highest because they capture real-world events as they happen — Twitter posts from officials and journalists break news before markets price it in. Market and OrderFlow weights are lower because they reflect crowd positioning, which lags actual events.

DeskSourceWeightVisual
🕵️ Pulse X/Twitter: real-time posts from officials, verified analysts, CENTCOM, State Dept 0.40 (40%) — highest
📡 Beacon GDELT news events, credibility-scored RSS feeds from Reuters, AP, BBC, etc. 0.35 (35%) — second
📊 Market Polymarket price momentum, volume, trade flow — crowd consensus signal 0.15 (15%)
📈 OrderFlow Live bid/ask depth, proximity-weighted imbalance — positioning intent 0.10 (10%)

Within each desk, individual signals are further multiplied by their importance_score (0–100) and a time decay factor (half-life 12 hours) so fresh high-importance signals from credible sources dominate the estimate.

Step 3 — Probability Aggregation Formula

Starting from the raw Polymarket YES price as the anchor, the model computes a weighted adjustment:

final_prob = market_price + Σ( signal.probability_impact × (signal.importance / 100) × desk_weight ) Normalised to [0.01, 0.99] to prevent extreme values.

The confidence of the final estimate is derived from the degree of convergence across desks — if all four desks point the same direction, confidence is high. If desks disagree, confidence is reduced proportionally.

Step 4 — Order Book Proximity Weighting

Raw bid/ask dollar depth is misleading if most orders are far from the mid-price. We apply a proximity discount so that a $1,000 ask at 99¢ is nearly worthless when the mid-price is 8¢, while a $1,000 ask at 9¢ counts nearly in full:

proximity_weight(price) = max(0, 1 − |price − mid| × 2) Examples when mid = 8¢: ask at 9¢ → weight = max(0, 1 − |0.09 − 0.08| × 2) = 0.98 (near full weight) ask at 20¢ → weight = max(0, 1 − |0.20 − 0.08| × 2) = 0.76 ask at 50¢ → weight = max(0, 1 − |0.50 − 0.08| × 2) = 0.16 ask at 99¢ → weight = max(0, 1 − |0.99 − 0.08| × 2) = 0.00 (zero weight)

Weighted bid depth vs weighted ask depth then determines the order book bias signal fed into the final estimate.

Step 5 — Pulse Author Credibility Scoring

Not all tweets are equal. Each tweet author is scored 0–100 based on three factors:

FactorBoostLogic
Pre-known account tier38–95 baseDoD/CENTCOM/POTUS = 95; Reuters/AP = 88; OSINT analysts = 65–72; unknown = 45
Follower count+0 to +375M+ followers floors score at 82; 100K+ at 63; <10K no boost
Blue verified badge+8Capped at 95
Official bio keywords+5 or +10"general", "pentagon", "minister", "department" etc. in bio description

Tweets from official sources (credibility ≥ 85) carry ~8× the weight of unverified accounts. A single tweet from @DeptofDefense about Iran can shift the estimate more than 50 tweets from random accounts.

Step 6 — Beacon Source Credibility

News articles from GDELT and RSS feeds are scored by domain. Headlines from Reuters, AP, BBC, NYT anchor at high credibility. GDELT tone scores (−100 to +100, where −100 is maximally negative/conflict) are mapped to probability impact. A GDELT tone of −10 on a CENTCOM article raises the YES probability significantly; a tone of +5 on a press-release domain barely moves it.

Step 7 — Bet YES / Bet NO / Hold Decision

After computing final_prob and comparing it to the live market price, the system issues a recommendation:

ConditionRecommendationLogic
final_prob > market_price + 3pp AND confidence ≥ 55BET YESModel sees YES as underpriced — buy YES shares
final_prob < market_price − 3pp AND confidence ≥ 55BET NOModel sees YES as overpriced — buy NO shares
|final_prob − market_price| < 3pp OR confidence < 55HOLD / MONITOREdge is within noise; wait for clearer signal

The 3pp threshold prevents over-trading on small noise. As confidence grows (more convergent signals), the threshold effectively shrinks because the estimate becomes more trustworthy. In URGENT mode (multiple high-importance escalation signals from official sources), the system lowers the threshold to 2pp and flags the alert level.

Step 8 — Alert Levels

The alert level summarises the overall signal environment, independent of the bet direction:

LevelMeaning
URGENTHigh-importance signals from official/verified sources pointing to imminent escalation. Maximum confidence. War room mode.
IMPORTANTMultiple corroborating signals from semi-confirmed sources. Elevated attention warranted.
DIGESTNormal signal flow. Background intelligence gathering active, no immediate edge.
NO ACTIONInsufficient signals or all desks quiet. Model defers to market price.
